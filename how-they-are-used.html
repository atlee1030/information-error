How They
Are Used

Metal

Metals, like glass, can be melted down and recycled infinitely, and production through recycling is actually more cost-effective.

Glass

Unlike plastic, glass cannot be reborn into various forms. However, glass is suitable for true recycling because it can be melted down during the recycling process and reborn infinitely as the same glass without any loss in quality.

Paper

Paper can be recycled 5 to 7 times due to quality degradation with each recycling cycle. It can be recycled more times than plastic, which degrades in quality, because paper retains usability in many forms even after degradation.

Organic

Organic materials may be difficult to categorize in terms of recycling cycles. Rather than recycling, they are closer to returning to nature, as they can be sent back through composting or used to grow crops.

Plastic

Plastic can be recycled into various materials. The most commonly known examples are recycled plastic bottles or plastic bags. Each recycling process, however, degrades its quality, often limiting reuse to about one or two cycles.
